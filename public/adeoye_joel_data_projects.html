Key policy events
🔴 1986 — SAP deregulation. Price rose from ₦0.20 to ₦0.39 (first major subsidy cut).
🔴 2012 — Jonathan subsidy removal attempt caused nationwide protests. Price briefly hit ₦141 before reversal to ₦97.
🔴 2023 — Tinubu inauguration: "subsidy is gone." Price jumped from ₦184 → ₦617 overnight, then to ₦897 by Q4 2024.
📉 Naira depreciation correlation: every 10% naira decline maps to ~8.3% fuel price increase (import cost pass-through).

📊 Food & non-alcoholic beverages account for ~52% of the CPI basket — making food prices the dominant inflation driver.
📊 The Phillips Curve (inflation ↓ = unemployment ↓) does NOT hold in Nigeria. Both rose together post-2015, pointing to structural unemployment unlinked to monetary cycles.
📊 Youth unemployment persists above 50% even during low-inflation periods (2013–2016), indicating supply-side structural barriers, not demand-side causes.
